SuttaCentral aims to facilitate the study of Buddhist scriptures from comparative and historical perspectives. It caters for those who wish to deepen their understanding of Buddhism through careful study of the earliest scriptural sources.

The emphasis is on the canonical texts that were compiled by the early schools during the first five centuries after the Buddha's Parinirvana. These scriptures, representing the shared scriptural heritage of Buddhism, are preserved mainly in Pali and Chinese, with smaller amounts in Sanskrit and Tibetan. They may be classified as follows:

  • Nikāyas/Āgamas, the major collections of discourses (suttas/sutras);
  • Various minor works, collections of verses, and miscellaneous discourses;
  • The Vinaya texts;
  • Certain portions of the canonical Abhidhamma works.

Much of this material is already available on the web. SuttaCentral aims to co-ordinate the existing web-based resources through correspondence tables, and to provide access to additional texts and translations that are otherwise not readily available.

The correspondence tables list the texts that have been identified as "parallels" or "counterparts" between the various collections. They focus primarily on the Nikāyas/Āgamas, though we hope eventually to extend their scope to cover all early Buddhist material. Referring to the correspondence tables enables one to locate Chinese, Sanskrit, or Tibetan parallel(s) to a given Pali sutta, or vice versa, and is the natural starting point in navigating around this mass of textual material.

Recognising the importance of language in such studies, we also include some resources to help those who may wish to learn the textual languages found on this site.
